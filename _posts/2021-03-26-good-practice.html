Flocks of birds

A physics joke tells that in order to describe a cow's milk production we can model it as a sphere, but this is not far from the truth. In our description a bird is a triangle wandering in a 2-dimensional world togheter with its mates and it is carachterized by a certain velocity vector. In this first part of the analysis each bird will have the same velocity in magnitude of 1 m/s and just different orientations. To understand how ordered flocks emerge from the behaviour of single birds we first need a quantity to quantify the amount of order in our flock. We introduce the so called order parameter tha twe calculate using the following procedure: we sum usingthe head tail technque all the birds velocity vectors and divide the length of the resulting vector by the number of birds in our flock. This final length measured in units of m/s is the order parameter, now let's see how this quantity gives us information about the amount of order in our grop of birds.
